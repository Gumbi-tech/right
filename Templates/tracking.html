First-Aid Instructions

To perform CPR, place the heel of one hand on the center of the person's chest. Place the other hand on top and press down hard and fast at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute. Allow the chest to rise fully between compressions. If trained, provide rescue breaths after every 30 compressions.

For minor burns, run the affected area under cool (not cold) water for 10-15 minutes. Avoid using ice. Cover the burn with a sterile, non-stick bandage or cloth. For severe burns, seek immediate medical attention.

Perform the Heimlich maneuver by standing behind the person and wrapping your arms around their waist. Make a fist, place it just above their navel, and perform quick inward and upward thrusts until the object is expelled. For infants, give 5 back slaps followed by 5 chest thrusts.

To stop heavy bleeding, apply direct pressure on the wound with a clean cloth or bandage. If the cloth becomes soaked, add more layers without removing the previous ones. Keep the injured limb elevated above the heart if possible. Seek emergency medical help if the bleeding does not stop.

If someone is experiencing a heart attack, have them sit down and remain calm. Call emergency services immediately. If they have prescribed nitroglycerin, help them take it. If the person becomes unresponsive, start CPR if necessary.
